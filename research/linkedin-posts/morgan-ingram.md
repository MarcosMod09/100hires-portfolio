### Content

A previous AE burned the account with years of low-value outreach.

Now prospects say:
“You’ve been calling me for years. I’m not interested.”

Instead of trying to fix that directly:

→ Find a new path.

Key plays:

1. Target new people in the account  
→ recently hired or promoted roles  

2. Leverage warm connections  
→ use internal network + Sales Navigator  

3. Stop selling — start enabling  
→ provide value first (no CTA)  

Core idea:
Rebuild trust by changing the approach, not forcing the same one harder.

---

### Insights

- Bad outbound damages long-term trust with accounts  
- Trying to “fix” a burned account directly often creates more resistance  
- New stakeholders = new opportunity paths  
- Trust can be borrowed through internal or external relationships  
- Value-first outreach rebuilds credibility faster than pitching  

---

### Actionable Tactics

- Identify:
  - new hires  
  - recently promoted stakeholders  
- Use Sales Navigator:
  - “Connections Of” filter for warm paths  
- Replace cold pitch with:
  - useful resources  
  - demos without pressure  
- Send value-first messages:
  - no CTA initially  
- Track engagement:
  - repeated views = buying intent  

---

### My Takeaways

- This adds a **recovery strategy** to outbound systems  
- Complements:
  - Berman → lead generation  
  - Braun → psychology  
  - Armand → execution  
  - Morgan → relationship rebuilding  
- For ModlyAI:
  → useful when approaching brands already contacted or oversold  
- Key idea:
  → outbound is not just starting conversations — it’s rebuilding trust when it’s broken

## Post 2

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/morganjingramamp_i-was-working-with-an-sdr-team-in-our-last-activity-7452463846277009408-Aaow?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFzJJ1sBCA1W7yrq47kyaYtHMd3I5V5RJrU

### Content

An SDR team was sending one LinkedIn message per prospect… and getting no responses.

The fix was simple:
→ follow up.

Instead of treating outreach like a one-shot attempt:

- send a second message  
- change the angle  
- use different formats  

Examples:
- video message  
- screenshot with insight  
- GIF  

Result:
→ 10 conversations from prospects that previously didn’t respond  

Framework:
Think of outreach like baseball:
- Strike 1 → initial message (personalized)  
- Strike 2 → new format (video/voice)  
- Strike 3 → lighter touch (GIF / humor)  

Core idea:
→ 3 attempts per prospect before moving on  

---

### Insights

- Most outreach fails due to **lack of follow-up, not bad messaging**
- Teams treat LinkedIn like a **one-shot channel instead of a process**
- Format variation increases engagement more than repeating the same message  
- Persistence creates opportunity from “dead” prospects  
- Consistency in follow-ups is a major pipeline driver  

---

### Actionable Tactics

- Set a rule:
  → minimum 3 touches per prospect  
- Vary format across attempts:
  - text → video → GIF  
- Change messaging angle each time:
  - insight → value → light engagement  
- Track follow-up rate per rep  
- Combine channels:
  - LinkedIn + email + phone  

---

### My Takeaways

- This adds **process consistency** to outbound strategy  
- Complements:
  - Berman → systems  
  - Braun → psychology  
  - Armand → execution  
  - Morgan → persistence + process  
- For ModlyAI:
  → build a structured follow-up system instead of one-off outreach  
- Key idea:
  → pipeline comes from persistence, not just good first messages

## Post 3

### Content

A simple AI-driven outbound strategy:

Start with the company’s **careers page**.

Job postings reveal:
- what the company is struggling with  
- where they’re investing  
- what problems they need solved  

Process:

1. Identify job titles that signal demand  
→ e.g. hiring SDRs → need pipeline  
→ hiring AEs with self-sourcing → need lead generation  

2. Use AI (Claude) to analyze job descriptions  
→ extract pain points  
→ identify decision makers  
→ generate personalized observations  

3. Send a LinkedIn message with:
→ a screenshot of the job post  
→ a tailored insight  

Core idea:
→ connect real signals (jobs) to outreach  

---

### Insights

- Job postings are **high-intent buying signals**
- Most outbound ignores publicly available context  
- AI is most valuable when used to **analyze, not generate blindly**
- Personalization works best when tied to **real company activity**
- The advantage is not AI itself — it’s how well you connect data points  

---

### Actionable Tactics

- Define trigger roles that indicate demand for your product  
- Monitor:
  - LinkedIn Jobs  
  - Indeed  
  - company careers pages  
- Use AI to:
  - extract pain points  
  - identify stakeholders  
- Build outreach messages using:
  - real signals (job posts)  
- Include proof:
  - screenshots or references to show research  

---

### My Takeaways

- This adds **signal-based outbound** to the system  
- Complements:
  - Berman → systems  
  - Braun → psychology  
  - Armand → execution  
  - Morgan → signals + AI + targeting  
- For ModlyAI:
  → target furniture companies hiring marketing/ecommerce roles

- Key idea:
  → the best outbound comes from connecting real-world signals to personalized messaging
