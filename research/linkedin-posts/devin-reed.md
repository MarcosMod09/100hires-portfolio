### Content

A CMO asks if newsletter sponsorships are worth it.

Answer:
→ yes, but only with the right strategy.

Key principles:

- Lead with an educational offer, not a product  
- Focus on engagement (click-to-open rate), not list size  
- Subject line determines targeting and performance  
- Start with curiosity, not product positioning  

Core idea:
Newsletter ads work when **strategy + targeting + copy** align.

---

### Insights

- Distribution channels fail when paired with poor offers  
- Engagement quality matters more than audience size  
- Copy (especially the first lines) has outsized impact  
- Buyers prefer learning over being sold to  

---

### Actionable Tactics

- Use lead magnets:
  - reports  
  - guides  
- Validate audience quality:
  - ask for CTR / engagement metrics  
- Optimize subject lines for targeting  
- Write openings that:
  - create curiosity  
  - avoid product-first messaging  

---

### My Takeaways

- This reinforces a key outbound idea:
  → value-first messaging outperforms direct selling  
- Applies to outreach:
  → lead with insight, not pitch  
- For ModlyAI:
  → offer design guides or visualization insights before pushing product
