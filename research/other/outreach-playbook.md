# Cold Outreach Playbook

This playbook outlines a structured outbound system based on research from top practitioners:
Alex Berman, Josh Braun, Guillaume Moubeche, Armand Farrokh, and Morgan J Ingram.

The goal is to build a repeatable system that generates pipeline through targeted, personalized, and consistent outreach.

---

## Step 1 — Targeting (Morgan J Ingram)

### Core Idea
Pipeline starts with targeting the right accounts using **real signals**, not random lists.

### Strategy
Use high-intent signals to identify companies that are more likely to need your solution.

### Tactics
- Identify trigger roles:
  - Hiring SDRs → need pipeline
  - Hiring AEs with self-source → need lead generation
- Analyze:
  - Careers pages
  - LinkedIn Jobs
  - Company growth signals
- Use AI (Claude/Codex) to extract:
  - pain points
  - decision makers
  - personalization angles

### Key Insight
> The best outbound comes from connecting real-world signals to messaging.

---

## Step 2 — Messaging (Alex Berman)

### Core Idea
Messaging should be clear, direct, and focused on results.

### Framework
Use simple structures:
- Problem → Impact → Solution (PIS)
- Personalization → Context → Call to action (PPC)

### Tactics
- Keep messages short and specific
- Focus on one clear outcome
- Avoid generic templates
- Tie message to a real signal (job post, activity, etc.)

### Example
"Noticed you're hiring SDRs — usually means pipeline pressure.  
We help teams increase qualified meetings without increasing headcount."

### Key Insight
> Clarity beats creativity in outbound.

---

## Step 3 — Tone & Psychology (Josh Braun)

### Core Idea
Remove pressure. Increase trust.

### Principles
- Detach from outcome
- Avoid desperation
- Focus on helping, not selling

### Tactics
- Replace:
  - "Just checking in"
  with:
  - new insight or angle
- Pause before sending follow-ups:
  → “Is this emotional or strategic?”
- Let prospects make decisions without pressure

### Key Insight
> People respond to calm, not neediness.

---

## Step 4 — Execution (Armand Farrokh)

### Core Idea
Execution is structured, not improvised.

### Systems

#### 1. Multi-Touch Outreach
- Minimum 3 touches per prospect:
  - Message 1 → personalized text
  - Message 2 → different format (video/voice)
  - Message 3 → lighter touch (GIF or simple message)

#### 2. Format Variation
- Text
- Video
- Screenshot
- Voice note

#### 3. Team Selling (if applicable)
- Assign roles:
  - Main Event (driver)
  - Storyteller
  - Bad Guy
  - Room Reader

### Key Insight
> Consistency and structure outperform random effort.

---

## Step 5 — Follow-Up System (Morgan J Ingram)

### Core Idea
Most pipeline comes from follow-ups, not first messages.

### Rules
- Never send just one message
- Follow up with a new angle each time
- Be “pleasantly persistent”

### Example Flow
1. Personalized message
2. Video follow-up
3. Insight or screenshot
4. Light engagement message

### Key Insight
> Outreach is a process, not a one-shot attempt.

---

## Step 6 — Personalization at Scale (Guillaume Moubeche)

### Core Idea
Personalization should show understanding, not just surface-level research.

### Strategy
- Focus on:
  - company challenges
  - role-specific problems
- Use AI to assist research, not replace thinking

### Tactics
- Reference:
  - hiring activity
  - company growth
  - recent changes
- Show insight before pitching

### Key Insight
> Signal > credentials. Insight > templates.

---

## Step 7 — Handling Burned Accounts (Morgan J Ingram)

### Problem
Accounts may be over-contacted or resistant.

### Solution
- Target new stakeholders
- Use warm connections
- Provide value first (no CTA)

### Tactics
- Send:
  - helpful resources
  - interactive demos
- Focus on rebuilding trust

### Key Insight
> You don’t fix bad outreach by doing more of it.

---

## Step 8 — Scaling the System

### Core Idea
Scale through systems, not brute force.

### Components
- Defined ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- Repeatable outreach process
- CRM tracking
- Performance metrics

### Metrics to Track
- Response rate
- Meeting rate
- Follow-up rate
- Conversion rate

### Key Insight
> Systems create consistency. Consistency creates pipeline.

---

## Final Takeaways

This outbound system is built on:

- Signal-based targeting
- Clear and relevant messaging
- Low-pressure communication
- Structured execution
- Consistent follow-up
- Smart use of AI

### Core Principle

> Outbound success comes from connecting the right message to the right person at the right time — consistently.

# Where Experts Disagree

## 1. Volume vs Personalization

- Alex Berman recommends scaling outreach with simple, repeatable messages sent at volume.  
  Source: Alex Berman, YouTube content on cold email systems, accessed April 2026  

- Josh Braun emphasizes thoughtful, low-pressure, highly personalized communication.  
  Source: Josh Braun, LinkedIn posts on sales psychology, accessed April 2026  

### My Position
I side with a hybrid approach:
- use signals for relevance (Morgan)
- keep messaging simple (Berman)
- maintain low-pressure tone (Braun)

Reason: Pure volume reduces quality, while over-personalization limits scalability.

---

## 2. Direct CTA vs Low-Pressure CTA

- Alex Berman often uses direct CTAs (“Want to explore?”)  
  Source: Alex Berman, LinkedIn posts, April 2026  

- Josh Braun recommends curiosity-based, low-pressure questions  
  Source: Josh Braun, LinkedIn posts, April 2026  

### My Position
Low-pressure CTAs perform better in early outreach.

Reason: Prospects are not ready to commit immediately. Reducing pressure increases response rate.

---

## 3. Automation vs Manual Research

- Morgan J Ingram uses AI and systems to scale targeting and personalization  
  Source: Morgan J Ingram, LinkedIn post on careers page strategy, April 2026  

- Guillaume Moubeche emphasizes human insight and positioning over automation  
  Source: Guillaume Moubeche, LinkedIn posts, April 2026  

### My Position
Use AI for research, not messaging.

Reason: AI is effective at identifying signals, but human judgment is required for relevance and tone.

---

# What I Rejected and Why

## 1. Generic Personalization (“Saw you work at X”)

Rejected because:
- low relevance
- easily identifiable as template
- does not create real engagement

Source: Observed across multiple experts emphasizing signal-based personalization (Morgan, Guillaume)

---

## 2. Single-Touch Outreach

Rejected because:
- most experts emphasize follow-ups
- significantly lowers response rates

Source: Morgan J Ingram, LinkedIn post on follow-up system, April 2026

# My Original Ideas

## Signal Timing Layer

Most outreach focuses on *what* signal to use.

My idea:
→ focus on *when* the signal appears

Example:
- reaching out immediately after a job post is published
- contacting newly promoted roles within 7–14 days

Why it could work:
- higher urgency
- lower competition
- better timing relevance

# Weaknesses of This Playbook

- Assumes access to tools like LinkedIn Sales Navigator or AI tools  
- Does not fully address industries with long sales cycles  
- Relies on accurate signal detection (which can be imperfect)  
- Does not include deep personalization at scale challenges
