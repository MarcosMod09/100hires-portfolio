# Josh Braun — Cold Outreach: Asking Better Questions & Detachment

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-yWiaoxvKQE

---

## Summary

This video focuses on improving cold outreach by shifting from pitching to diagnosing problems.  
Josh Braun emphasizes detachment, curiosity, and asking the right questions to uncover whether a real problem exists before introducing any solution.

---

## Key Outreach Strategies

- Replace pitching with **problem discovery**
- Ask **neutral, curiosity-driven questions** (“poke the bear”)
- Stay **detached from the outcome** to sound more natural
- Focus on **conversation over persuasion**
- Let the prospect talk — avoid over-explaining

---

## Core Concepts

### 1. “Sort, Don’t Sell”

Instead of assuming the prospect needs your product:
- Discover if a problem exists first  
- If no problem → move on  

This avoids forcing solutions where they’re not needed.

---

### 2. Detachment Changes Behavior

When your goal is:
- NOT to close  
- BUT to understand  

You:
- sound calmer  
- reduce pressure  
- increase trust  

Prospects can feel intent immediately. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

### 3. “Poke the Bear” Questions

Ask questions that:
- trigger curiosity  
- highlight potential problems  
- make the prospect think  

Example structure:
“When you’re doing [job], are you using A, B, or C?”

---

### 4. Problem First, Solution Later

Key principle:
> Solutions have no value without a problem

Never lead with:
- features  
- product pitch  

Only introduce solution AFTER problem is confirmed.

---

### 5. Loss Aversion

People are more motivated by:
- avoiding loss  
than  
- gaining benefits  

Frame problems as:
- risks  
- missed opportunities  

---

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Pitching too early  
- Sounding needy or desperate  
- Talking more than listening  
- Targeting the wrong audience  
- Using one message for all personas  

---

## Actionable Framework (Cold Call Flow)

1. Ask a neutral question about their process  
2. Let them explain how they currently solve it  
3. Ask:
   “How’s that been going?”  
4. Follow with:
   “You’ve probably considered [better approach]?”  
5. Stay silent and let them talk  

---

## Example Flow

- “When you handle [task], are you using spreadsheets, an in-house system, or something automated?”  
- “How’s that been going?”  
- “You’ve probably looked into automating it?”  

Then:
→ listen  
→ don’t pitch immediately
