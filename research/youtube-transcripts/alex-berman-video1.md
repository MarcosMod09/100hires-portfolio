# Alex Berman — Cold Email Formulas That Get Replies

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=881Dr4lMey4

---

## Summary

This video breaks down three proven cold email frameworks used to generate high reply rates (30–50%). 
Alex Berman focuses on simplicity, personalization, and psychological triggers rather than complex copywriting. 
The strategies are designed to drive responses and open conversations, not impress prospects.

---

## Key Outreach Strategies

- Use **simple frameworks** instead of writing emails from scratch  
- Personalization should be tied to a **specific, real pain point**  
- Optimize emails for **replies, not clicks or branding**  
- Keep messaging short and direct (2–4 lines preferred)  
- Use psychological triggers like **open loops** to increase curiosity  

---

## Core Frameworks

### 1. PC Formula (Pain → Call to Action)

- Identify a **specific pain point**
- Immediately follow with a **direct CTA**

**Example idea:**
“Hey [Name], noticed [specific issue].  
We built a tool to fix this — want more info?”

---

### 2. PEC Formula (Pain → Evidence → Call to Action)

- Highlight the prospect’s **desire or fear**
- Add **proof or case study**
- End with CTA

Focus:
- Emotional connection + logical proof

---

### 3. PPC Formula (Pain → Partial Solution → Call to Action)

- Show a pain point  
- Provide a **partial solution (teaser)**  
- CTA = “want the full solution?”

This creates curiosity and increases reply rates.

---

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Writing long, detailed emails  
- Being too generic (“we help companies grow”)  
- Over-personalizing inefficiently  
- Adding multiple CTAs or links  
- Trying to sound impressive instead of clear  

---

## Actionable Steps

1. Research one **specific pain point per prospect**  
2. Choose ONE framework (PC, PEC, or PPC)  
3. Write a short email (2–4 lines max)  
4. Include one clear CTA  
5. Test at scale and iterate based on replies
