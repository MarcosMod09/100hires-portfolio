# Armand Farrokh — Cold Call Script That Books 1/3 Meetings

Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cvwha9306V4

---

## Summary

This video breaks down a cold calling framework designed to consistently book meetings at a high rate (1 in 3 conversations).  
The approach focuses on reversing traditional sales tactics by reducing pressure, leading with problems, and guiding prospects through conversation instead of pitching.

---

## Key Outreach Strategies

- Use a **pattern-breaking opener** to avoid sounding like a telemarketer  
- Lead with a **specific problem**, not your product  
- Keep messaging simple and human (no jargon)  
- Avoid pushing meetings — instead, sell curiosity or interest  
- Handle objections by **agreeing first, then guiding conversation**  

---

## Core Framework

### 1. The Opener (Context-Based)

Avoid:
- “How’s your day going?”
- “Did I catch you at a bad time?”

Instead:
- Use context to sound familiar  

Example:
“Hey [Name], we work with other [similar role/company] — have you heard our name tossed around?”

Goal:
→ feel like a warm intro, not a cold call  

---

### 2. Problem Proposition (The Pitch)

Structure:
- Triggering problem (very specific)  
- Simple one-line solution  
- Low-pressure CTA  

Key idea:
👉 People care about problems, not features  

Example:
“Teams are getting flooded with 3,000+ applications and can’t screen efficiently.  
We use AI to surface the top candidates so you only review the best.  
Open to taking a look sometime?”

---

### 3. Objection Handling (Mr. Miyagi Method)

Instead of fighting objections:

#### Step 1 — Agree
Reduce pressure:
“I should have assumed you already had something in place…”

#### Step 2 — Ask Questions (Trap Questions)
Guide them to reveal gaps:
“Are you able to identify candidates with rapid promotions or specific experience?”

#### Step 3 — Sell the Test Drive
Not the product:
“Open to trying this on one role just to see how it performs?”

---

## Mistakes to Avoid

- Sounding like a telemarketer  
- Leading with product features  
- Using generic language (“optimize”, “streamline”)  
- Asking for a meeting too early  
- Fighting objections directly  

---

## Actionable Steps

1. Replace your opener with a context-based intro  
2. Define one **highly specific problem** your audience faces  
3. Write a one-sentence solution (simple language only)  
4. Use a low-pressure CTA (“open to exploring?”)  
5. When objections come:
   - agree  
   - ask questions  
   - offer a test  

---

## Key Insight

The biggest shift is:
**from pushing → guiding**

Top performers:
- don’t force decisions  
- create curiosity  
- let prospects discover problems themselves
